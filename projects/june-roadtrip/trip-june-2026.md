## Notes on Drive Days

**Shorter days (under 4 hours):**
- **June 7:** Russellville → OKC is only ~3 hrs. This is intentional — it's the first drive after two rest days, so it's an easy re-entry.
- **June 17:** Fresno → San Jose is ~2.5 hrs. This is a relaxed approach into the Bay Area.
- **June 18:** San Jose → SF is only ~1 hr. You're basically there.

**Longer days (over 5 hours):**
- **June 4:** Nashville → Russellville is ~5.5 hrs. Longest day until the Mojave crossing.
- **June 14:** Flagstaff → Barstow is ~5.5 hrs. Mojave Desert crossing — start early, bring water.

If you want to balance the load, you could shift ~50 miles from June 4 to June 3 (e.g., stop east of Nashville on June 3, then a shorter June 4). But the current split is practical for campgrounds.

## Detailed Leg Breakdown

### Leg 1: Atlanta, GA → Nashville, TN — June 3
- **Distance:** ~250 miles | **Drive time:** ~4.5 hours
- **Route:** I-75 north to I-24 west into Nashville
- **Stay:** Two Rivers RV Resort or Nashville KOA
- **Do:** Easy first day. Optional: Chattanooga stop (Lookout Mountain, Ruby Falls) if you leave early.

### Leg 2: Nashville, TN → Russellville, AR area — June 4
- **Distance:** ~330 miles | **Drive time:** ~5.5 hours
- **Route:** I-40 west across Tennessee, into Arkansas
- **Stay:** Dogwood Springs RV Park (Russellville area) or Ozark RV Park
- **Note:** Longest day until the Mojave. Arkansas River Valley, Ozark foothills.

### Leg 3: Russellville, AR area — June 5–6 (Rest Days)
- **Base:** Russellville / Fort Smith area
- **Do:**
  - Historic Fort Smith National Historic Site
  - Ozark National Forest — hiking, scenery
  - Lake Dardanelle (Arkansas River)
  - Downtown Russellville
- **Day trip:** Fort Smith is ~75 miles (~1.5 hrs) from Russellville

### Leg 4: Russellville, AR → Oklahoma City, OK — June 7
- **Distance:** ~180 miles | **Drive time:** ~3 hours
- **Route:** I-40 west
- **Stay:** Oklahoma City East KOA or Lake Hefner area RV parks
- **Note:** Short day — easy re-entry after rest days.

### Leg 5: Oklahoma City, OK → Amarillo, TX — June 8
- **Distance:** ~260 miles | **Drive time:** ~4 hours
- **Route:** I-40 west into Texas Panhandle
- **Stay:** Amarillo KOA or Lake Meredith National Recreation Area (free/cheap)

### Leg 6: Amarillo, TX area — June 9 (Rest Day)
- **Do:**
  - Palo Duro Canyon State Park (~30 min south) — "Grand Canyon of Texas," hiking, scenic drives
  - Cadillac Ranch (in Amarillo)
  - Big Texan Steak Ranch

### Leg 7: Amarillo, TX → Albuquerque, NM — June 10
- **Distance:** ~290 miles | **Drive time:** ~4.5 hours
- **Route:** I-40 west across New Mexico
- **Stay:** Albuquerque KOA or High Desert RV Park
- **Do:** Old Town Albuquerque evening stroll, green chile dinner
- **Note:** Pass through Tucumcari (Route 66 nostalgia), Santa Rosa Blue Hole

### Leg 8: Albuquerque, NM → Flagstaff, AZ — June 11
- **Distance:** ~320 miles | **Drive time:** ~5 hours
- **Route:** I-40 west
- **Stay:** Flagstaff KOA Holiday or Black Barts RV Park
- **Optional detour:** Petrified Forest National Park (adds ~1 hr)
- **Note:** You gain altitude — Flagstaff is 7,000 ft. Cool mountain air.

### Leg 9: Flagstaff, AZ area — June 12–13 (Rest Days)
- **Base:** Flagstaff
- **Do:**
  - **Grand Canyon day trip** — South Rim (~1.5 hrs north). Desert View Drive, Mather Point, Visitor Center.
  - **Sedona** — red rock country (~45 min south). Cathedral Rock, Bell Rock, Oak Creek Canyon.
  - **Lowell Observatory** — where Pluto was discovered. Night sky programs.
  - **Wupatki National Monument** — ancient pueblo ruins (~30 min north)
  - **Historic Route 66** — walkable downtown Flagstaff, breweries (Wanderlust, Dark Sky, Mother Road)
- **Note:** This is your longest linger of the trip. Use it well.

### Leg 10: Flagstaff, AZ → Barstow, CA — June 14
- **Distance:** ~350 miles | **Drive time:** ~5.5 hours
- **Route:** I-40 west across Mojave Desert
- **Stay:** Barstow/Calico KOA or Desert Springs RV Park
- **⚠️ June heat:** Start early (7 AM). Bring water. Run generator for AC if needed. This is the longest/hottest day.

### Leg 11: Barstow, CA → Fresno / Madera area — June 15
- **Distance:** ~280 miles | **Drive time:** ~4 hours
- **Route:** CA-58 west to CA-99 north (Central Valley)
- **Stay:** Fresno area RV park
- **Note:** Agricultural Central Valley. Less scenic but efficient.

### Leg 12: Fresno / Madera area — June 16 (Rest Day)
- **Base:** Fresno area
- **Do:**
  - **Sequoia National Park day trip** (~1 hr east). Can't take trailer on Generals Highway (winding/steep), but you can park at the visitor center and use the shuttle.
  - **Kings Canyon** — alternative to Sequoia, fewer crowds
  - Explore Fresno — Tower District, local food

### Leg 13: Fresno → San Jose / South Bay — June 17
- **Distance:** ~150 miles | **Drive time:** ~2.5 hours
- **Route:** CA-99 to CA-152 (Pacheco Pass) to US-101 or I-680
- **Stay:** San Jose area RV park (options limited — book ahead) or Coyote Valley RV Park
- **Note:** Short day. You're now in the Bay Area approach.

### Leg 14: San Jose → San Francisco — June 18
- **Distance:** ~50 miles | **Drive time:** ~1 hour
- **Route:** US-101 north or I-280 to San Francisco
- **Stay:** San Francisco North/Petaluma KOA Resort, Marin RV Park, or Pacifica RV Resort
- **Arrive:** Thursday afternoon/evening
- **Note:** Very short final leg. With a 25ft trailer, avoid driving in SF itself. Stay in Petaluma, Marin, or Pacifica and take ferry/BART/uber into the city.

### June 19 — Friday
- **In San Francisco** — first full day visiting Rachel
- No driving. You're here. ✅

## Camper-Specific Tips

1. **Elevation:** Flagstaff is 7,000 ft. If you're sensitive to altitude, take it easy first day there.
2. **Heat:** Mojave crossing (June 14) will be 95–100°F. Drive early, run generator for AC, hydrate.
3. **Fuel:** Don't let tank drop below 1/4 in New Mexico and Arizona — long stretches between stations.
4. **Reservations:** June is peak travel season. Book Flagstaff and Bay Area campgrounds ASAP.
5. **Bay Area:** Do NOT drive the trailer into San Francisco itself. Narrow streets, hills, no RV parking. Stay in Petaluma, Marin, or Pacifica.
