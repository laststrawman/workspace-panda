## EASTBOUND ROUTE (San Francisco → Atlanta)

### Leg 1: San Francisco → Redwoods (Avenue of the Giants)
- **Distance:** ~250 miles | **Drive time:** ~4 hrs
- **Stay:** Humboldt Redwoods SP — Burlington Campground (hookups available)
- **Do:** Walk among old-growth redwoods — genuinely awe-inspiring

### Leg 2: Redwoods → Crater Lake, OR
- **Distance:** ~350 miles | **Drive time:** ~5.5 hrs
- **Stay:** Mazama Campground inside Crater Lake NP (hookups, book way ahead)
- **Do:** The lake is one of the most stunning things you'll ever see

### Leg 3: Crater Lake → Bend, OR
- **Distance:** ~90 miles | **Drive time:** ~1.5 hrs
- Short hop, worth a day or two
- **Stay:** Tumalo State Park (full hookups, Deschutes River)
- **Do:** Great food/beer scene, lava tubes, Smith Rock

### Leg 4: Bend, OR → Boise, ID
- **Distance:** ~280 miles | **Drive time:** ~4 hrs
- **Stay:** Boise KOA or Fiesta RV Park (full hookups)
- Boise is underrated — good food, walkable downtown

### Leg 4b: Boise, ID → Island Park, ID ⭐
- **Distance:** ~220 miles | **Drive time:** ~3.5 hrs
- **⏰ Timing goal: arrive between July 1-24**
- **Visit:** Chapin and Ann Marie (friends who will be there that window)
- **Stay:** Flat Rock RV Park or Buffalo Campground (near Yellowstone west entrance)
- Island Park sits on the Henry's Fork — world-class fly fishing, stunning caldera views
- **Do:** Henry's Fork of the Snake River (canoe/kayak paradise), Mesa Falls, wildlife viewing
- 🦴 **Bonus:** Yellowstone is 30 min away — geysers, hot springs, bison, fossil forests
- 🎵 Island Park area has a laid-back mountain music scene worth exploring

### Leg 5: Island Park, ID → Salt Lake City, UT
- **Distance:** ~340 miles | **Drive time:** ~5 hrs
- **Stay:** Salt Lake City KOA (full hookups, easy access)
- **Do:** Great Salt Lake, Temple Square, gateway to the red rocks

### Leg 6: Salt Lake City → Moab, UT
- **Distance:** ~235 miles | **Drive time:** ~3.5 hrs
- **Stay:** Moab Valley RV Resort (full hookups, red rock views)
- **Do:** Arches NP, Canyonlands NP — absolutely do not skip this
- 🦴 **Fossil stop:** Mill Canyon Dinosaur Trail (free, near Moab) — trackways and bones in the open desert

### Leg 7: Moab → Denver, CO
- **Distance:** ~360 miles | **Drive time:** ~5.5 hrs
- **Stay:** Denver Meadows RV Park (full hookups, convenient)
- Optional: slow down through the Rockies
- 🦴 **Fossil stop:** **Dinosaur National Monument** (Jensen, UT / Dinosaur, CO) — a wall of embedded dinosaur bones you can literally touch. Slight detour but absolutely worth it.

### Leg 8: Denver → Kansas City or Oklahoma City
- **Distance:** ~600 miles | **Drive time:** ~8-9 hrs
- Split into 2 days
- Or cut south through Amarillo/Oklahoma — flatter but faster

### Leg 9: Oklahoma City → Atlanta
- **Distance:** ~760 miles | **Drive time:** ~11 hrs
- Split into 2 days — stop in Little Rock or Memphis
- **Memphis stop:** Graceland area RV parks, BBQ is mandatory

## Camper-Specific Tips

1. **Elevation:** Flagstaff is 7,000 ft. If you're sensitive to altitude, take it easy.
2. **Heat:** June in the Mojave (Leg 7) will be hot. Drive early, run the generator for AC if needed.
3. **Fuel:** Stretches in New Mexico and Arizona can be long. Don't let the tank drop below 1/4.
4. **Reservations:** June is peak travel season. Book campgrounds ahead, especially weekends.
5. **I-40 Condition:** Generally excellent for RVs. Few steep grades compared to I-10 or northern routes.
